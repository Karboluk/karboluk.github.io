Helios is a Major God

Regional Names:

Lux (The Luxan Empire)

Domains:

Death, Life, Light, Order, War

Regional Worship:

Lawbringers (Thracia). Most current mortals worship his Aspects rather than Helios directly.

Nature:

The Luxan's eponymous God of the Sun, Helios is the paragon of truth, justice, and beauty. He believes in progress, the improvement of mortal lives and the progression of their collective skills. Helios and his coterie, the Muses, are directly involved in the arts, medicine, and the ingenuity of invention.

Helios has a deep and unflagging love of mortals, to the point that I suspect he created them to begin with. For this reason, he is one of the few Major Gods active in the mortal realm (through his muses and other agents, and his own distant presence, of course). Helios often interferes to change things as he thinks they should be changed; or enlists mortal heroes to do the same, whether they are willing or not.

Associated Minor Deities/Aspect Deities:

Aesclepius, the Mender, Muse of Healing
Aesthenes, the Glorious, Muse of Beauty
Parvati, the Accepting, Muse of Love

Relations to other Deities:

Origins:

The second oldest of the Major Gods, it's known from archaeological evidence that Helios was venerated by The Luxan Empire, meaning he predates the first major civilization. Given his remit, it is likely that he gifted civilization to mortals, and The Luxan Empire was the result.

Helios, unlike the Earth Mother and the other Elder Gods, is known to have Ascended, which is why he is classified as a Major God; the Winds have made this fact clear, but refuse to discuss it further. Due to the passage of time and the Sundering of the Lands, there is no record of the catastrophe caused by his Ascent to Godhood, but the lines of power that run below the ancient Luxan sites may be evidence of what happened given their veneration of the Sun God.

It is generally assumed that Helios is, in all real meanings of the word, the Sun.

Activity:

Helios above almost all else loves

Recently, Helios is suspected to be involved in the creation of The Dry Lands and The Wastes of Subdurni, both in The Eastern Continent in or near The Shining Vale. His motives for this activity are not known, but adherents of him and his muses are common in the area, and a child of his, Callamus the Righteous, conquered The Shining Vale in DR 570, leaving an occupied puppet state in place.

Desired Behaviour in Followers:

Helios rewards ambition, the pursuit of knowledge, the pursuit of justice and the appreciation for the good things in life.

Piety Benefits:

You increase your piety score to Helios when you expand the god’s influence in the world in a concrete way through acts such as these:

  • Carrying out punishment on a fugitive from justice
  • Exacting vengeance for a significant wrong done to you
  • Defending a settlement from attacking monsters
  • Commissioning great works of Art
  • Taking a great personal risk to discover something

Your piety score to Helios decreases if you diminish Helios’s influence in the world, contradict his ideals, or make him look weak and cowardly through acts such as these:

  • Breaking an explicit promise or oath
  • Violating any just law
  • Putting others at risk through your own cowardice
  • Rejection of a path forward because the potential for harm

-Helios’s Devotee Piety 3+ Helios trait. As a devotee of Helios, you have proven yourself a worthy champion of the sun god. You can call on Helios’s favor and cast bless with this trait, requiring no material components. Helios’s blessing manifests as a nimbus around the affected creatures, causing them to shed dim light in a 5-foot radius until the spell ends. You can cast the spell in this way a number of times equal to your Wisdom modifier (minimum of once) or with spell slots. You regain all expended free uses when you finish a long rest. Wisdom is your spellcasting ability for this spell. 
-Helios’s Votary Piety 10+ Helios trait You can cast daylight with this trait once, or with spell slots. You regain your free use when you finish a long rest. Wisdom is your spellcasting ability for this spell. In addition, you have advantage on saving throws against being frightened. 
-Helios’s Disciple Piety 25+ Helios trait You grow accustomed to the blazing radiance of the sun; you have advantage on saving throws against being blinded, and you have resistance to fire damage. 
-Champion of the Sun Piety 50+ Helios trait You can increase your Strength or Wisdom score by 2 and also increase your maximum for that score by 2.
