Thalassus is a Major God

Regional Names:

Argos (Thracia), The Drowning One, The Depths which Remember, Wise Uncle

Domains:

Knowledge,Nature,Tempest

Regional Worship:

Travellers (Thracia), Sounders (Redhill, The Shining Vale)

Nature:

The God of the Sea, Thalassus is the deep darkness, the constant provider and also the fickle destroyer. A source of constant slow change and renewal, Thalassus is a patron of underwater animals and peoples, druids, fishermen, explorers, sailors, and traders. Conservative minded sages often invoke the name of Thalassus, or his regional name, when urging caution in experiments.

Thalassus guides his followers safely to distant shores, providing the proper offerings are made, and confounds the ships of the unfaithful and disrespectful. Thalassus is constant and less active in the affairs of the world, as his position can not be contested even by the other Gods. He is older than most of the Gods, and rich with the ancient knowledge he has collected, and sees efforts from the other gods to impose order as folly.

Associated Minor Deities/Aspect Deities:

Anuket, the Flowing Water, Aspect of Collecting
Aethenius, the Scholar, Aspect of Knowledge

Relations to other Deities:

Thalassus and Volkanus are in active opposition, and their followers will sometimes become violent.

Origins:

The original mortal ruler, King Apsu-Toli ruling the Bassarian culture, Thalassus's Ascent to Godhood caused the Sundering of the Lands, an event which split the continents apart- filling the voids left behind with the majesty of his waters. This is the first time that the ascent of a Major God created catastrophe, but this has been the most common result since.

Desired Behavior in Followers:

You increase your piety score to Thalassus when you expand the god’s influence in the world in a concrete way through acts such as these:

  • Supporting those who would reform or overturn institutions
  • Preventing cataclysmic change
  • Offering a treasure to the sea
  • Defending or maintaining a temple to Thalassus

Your piety score to Thalassus decreases if you diminish Thalassus’s influence in the world, contradict his ideal of gradual change, or attempt to impose artificial order through acts such as these:

  • Trying to keep a secret from Thalassus
  • Using magic to calm the sea’s fury
  • Upholding an institution not devoted to Thalassus
  • Bowing to the desires or demands of another god

Piety Benefits:

-Thalassus’s Devotee Piety 3+ Thalassus trait. As a devotee of Thalassus, you have proven yourself a worthy representative of the god of the sea. You learn fog cloud with this trait. Fog created in this way smells strongly of the sea. You can cast the spell a number of times equal to your Intelligence modifier (minimum of once), or with a spell slot. You regain all expended free uses when you finish a long rest. Intelligence is your spellcasting ability for this spell. 
-Thalassus’s Votary Piety 10+ Thalassus trait. You learn blink with this trait. You can cast the spell in this way once, or with a spell slot. You regain the free use when you finish a long rest. Intelligence is your spellcasting ability for this spell. 
-Thalassus’s Disciple Piety 25+ Thalassus trait. You are inspired by the tempestuous, uncontrollable nature of the sea; you have advantage on saving throws against being charmed or restrained. 
-Champion of Victory Piety 50+ Thalassus trait. You can increase your Dexterity or Intelligence score by 2 and also increase your maximum for that score by 2.
