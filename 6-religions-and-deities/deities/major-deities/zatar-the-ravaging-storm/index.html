Zatar is a Major God

Regional Names:

Zatar's established faith has not yet had time to spread so much that the name is lost.

Domains:

Death, Tempest, War

Regional Worship:

Established worship is, as of yet, limited to tribal groups and fanatic cults. This is expected to change over the coming centuries.

Nature:

Zatar is the cruel and vicious Goddess of carnage, destruction and storms. Tearing her way through the world with no goals beyond death and disarray, she is a force that most of the other Gods defend against in concert, turning her away to wreak havoc elsewhere.

Her adherents, the Bloodbound, are not welcome in polite society, and generally do not hide themselves there; they are the wild barbarian, the vengeful druid, the destructive spellcaster, and others who simply wish to kill, kill, kill for no reason other than their own drives.

Rumour says she is also the source of lycanthropy, though this remains unconfirmed and would fly in the face of known history. Nice embellishment, though. - Reyvan

Associated Minor Deities/Aspect Deities:

Relations to other Deities:

Zatar has relentless aggression towards Maahes, which is likely competition for the same pool of adherents.

Despite favorable motions from Volkanus towards Zatar and on her benefit, there has been no notable accommodations in the other direction.

I assume, but have not yet observed, that when Rrakash becomes more well known, he and Zatar will either join forces, given their shared goals and prior hatred of necromancers, or attempt to tear each other apart. It's quite possible that both will happen, which will leave spoils for those like me. -Reyvan

Origins:

Zatar's Ascent to Godhood is the most clearly documented, as the more recent ascensions were only witnessed by a few adventurers, who were more or less causal in the path they took.

In life, Isthariel the Channeler was likely the fifth or sixth most powerful wizard on the mortal plane, hindered more by her lack of subtlety than her actual prowess. See her entry for more detail, but the end result was the Fall of Cimmeria and the end of Cimmerian Empire, along with a few dead and/or tortured Minor Gods.

It is my personal belief, based on a few decades of study, that the Fall of Cimmeria, essentially a mithril hammer striking the ground with immense force, is what has disrupted the carefully laid, and centuries-long neglected, lines of power of The Luxan Empire in what is now called The Shining Vale, Subdurni, Lorica, The High Depths and The Fernish Jungle. Please see the entry on Callamus the Righteous and his conquest of the Shining Vale for more details. - Reyvan

Activity:

Zatar has been very active since her ascension.

Desired Behaviour in Followers:

Zatar wishes for chaotic destruction, and nothing less. Her followers tend toward raiding, razing, ruining everything in their local environment. Among their own people, contests are often brutal and lethal.

Piety Benefits:

You increase your piety score to Zatar when you expand the goddess’s influence in the world in a concrete way through acts such as these:
 - Defeating a champion of Maahes
 - Taking vengeance against a powerful foe
 - Burning a polis or a settlement to the ground
 - Desecrating a temple to Maahes as an offering to Zatar
 
 Your piety score to Zatar decreases if you diminish Zatar’s influence in the world, contradict her ideals, or make her look weak or ineffectual through acts such as these:
 - Failing to carry out a sworn vendetta
 - Publicly displaying weakness or compassion
 - Rejecting a duel or a challenge out of fear

-Zatar’s Devotee Piety 3+ Zatar trait. As a devotee of Zatar, you have shown yourself to be a brutal combatant. You can call on Zatar’s favor and cast wrathful smite with this trait. Zatar’s blessing manifests as a blue glow around your weapon, causing it to shed dim light in a 5-foot radius until the spell ends. You can cast the spell in this way a number of times equal to your Constitution modifier (minimum of once), or with spell slots. You regain all expended free uses when you finish a long rest. Constitution is your spellcasting ability for this spell.

-Zatar’s Votary Piety 10+ Zatar trait. You can cast blinding smite with this trait once per long rest, or with spell slots. Constitution is your spellcasting ability for this spell.

-Zatar’s Disciple Piety 25+ Zatar trait. Countless battles and vicious visions have given you focus. You have advantage on saving throws against being charmed or frightened, or immunity if you already had advantage.

-Champion of Slaughter Piety 50+ Zatar trait. You can increase your Strength or Constitution score by 2 and also increase your maximum for that score by 2.
