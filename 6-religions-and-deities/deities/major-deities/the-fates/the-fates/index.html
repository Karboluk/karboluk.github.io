The Fates are Major Gods

Regional Names:

Most often simply referred to as "The Fates" collectively, there are some references that name them as "The Grey Sisters", "Maiden-Mother-Crone", or "The Moirai". I believe I even came across a Bassarian reference calling them "Those stupid bitch gods", but I couldn't find a second piece of writing from that author, so they may simply have been in a bad mood.

Domains:

Each of the Fates has their own Domain list.

Regional Worship:

The Fates are more widely recognized than most other Ascended Gods, but are not strongly worshipped. It is common to find a temple or a shrine to the untended. In the case of Thracia, and in isolated places elsewhere, one may find at a temple a collection of The Fatebound; these priestesses (for they are always women) will always contain, support, and replace, a triad of seers.

Nature:

The Fates, deemed to be in control of the destinies of all living things and possibly of the Gods themselves, are always depicted as three sisters; Clotho , Lachesis and Atropos . Clotho spins the strands of Fate, Lachesis weaves its tapestry, and Atropos cuts the threads at their end; as such, they represent the beginning and end of life and everything in between.

The Fates, like The Earth Mother, are omnipresent in the activity of mortals. Whether this counts as activity or as passive observation depends largely on your opinion on the existence of free will.

Associated Minor Deities/Aspect Deities:

Relations to other Deities:

The Fates seem to have a strained relationship with themselves sometimes- not the individual sisters, mind you, but each fate with themself. Lachesis particularly suffers from this, but the tribulations happen so far above the mortal (and indeed, immortal) point of view as to be indecipherable.

Origins:

See the individual Fates

Activity:

As described above, do the Fates meddle? Are they active? Better minds than mine have gone insane trying to puzzle that out. They are certainly knowledgeable, and seem to know what they are doing....and seem to, in fact, be doing things.

Desired Behaviour in Followers:

See the individual Fates

Piety Benefits:

Clerics of the Fates align with one of the three aspects, and often serve as Diviners to nobility.

Unlike with clerics of other deities, it is very natural for an adherent to progress through worship of each Fate in order through their life. Any Piety gained when the time comes is kept, though the exiting rewards are lost when the new ones are gained.
