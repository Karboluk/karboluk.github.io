If Helios would teach mortals to put fire behind glass and have a lantern, for example, Volkanus is just as likely to teach them to put fire into bread, so they could cut open a loaf and eat toast in the morning. Volkanus is a Major God

Regional Names:

Moradin (Dwarven Cultural), St. Isolde (The Shining Vale VR 120-PI),

Domains:

Forge, Knowledge, War

Regional Worship:

Among human cultures, worship of Volkanus is generally either as a Fire Cult (Thracia), individual eccentrics, or a proper human-lead church as in The Shining Vale. Highly variable, they could be anywhere- but the signs of madness that tend to follow them aren't difficult to spot.

Among dwarves, he is venerated as their father; they work tirelessly to create makings worthy of him.

Gnomes aren't uncommon in either the human or dwarven worship scenarios, as their natural curiosity lends well to the practice. In both cases, though, they seem to succumb to the more manic expressions of interest and faith.

My personal favourite is in the Vale, because they don't seem to notice or care that they are venerating a Gnomish Saint that isn't dead and who actively protested the use of her name and story, in person, before giving up and straight-up leaving time. Priceless. -Reyvan

Nature:

Volkanus is the god of the forge, the restless earth, and fire. He rules the raw creative force that infuses sapient minds. Volkanus is also the god of artisans, obsession, mania, and the cycle of creation and destruction.

As a forge radiates heat in the area around it, Volkanus’s influence provides inspiration to mortals. He makes exquisitely crafted objects almost constantly, sometimes absentmindedly working while he holds conversations with the other gods, only to destroy the finished product and begin again. Impulsive and mercurial, Volkanus is prone to bouts of either joyous productivity or frustrated anger. He often feels constrained by the limits of imagination, yearning to realize ideas that seem just out of reach.

Volkanus’s preferred form is that of a muscular man whose coal-hued skin is mostly covered in mutable organic bronze. He might also appear in the form of a fiery phoenix or a bull made of cooling lava, and for that reason, both of those creatures are associated with him. When angered, he might appear as an enormous mass of lava, a blazing fire, or a volcanic eruption. Mortals who see Volkanus in one of those forms seldom live to tell about it.

Associated Minor Deities/Aspect Deities:

Dun, the Smelter, Aspect of Labour
Lady Racca, the Planner, Aspect of Construction

Relations to other Deities:

Volkanus, nearly alone among the gods, is an advocate for Zatar the Ravaging Storm. I assume it's because the destruction leaves so many more pieces for Volkanus to work with.

Volkanus and Thalassus are in active opposition, and their followers will sometimes become violent.

Origins:

Volkanus arose in Thracia during the height of Maahes' influence, as the land was being shattered and splintered by his presence. In a relatively peaceful Ascent to Godhood, the stories say that the large volcano, named for the God, of course, that grew between the warring cities of Agaria and Brekaris was the beginning of the end for Maahes's time on this realm; when he saw how much damage he was causing to such an inferior people, his shame demanded his retreat. Volkanus rose up, and The Shield of Thracia, the mountain range now present, sealed almost a year of peace.

Land was filled in and several nearby islands were rejoined to the mainland, which likely began the feud with Thalassus, but Volkanus did not remain on this realm in person. He uses his Minor Gods and Aspects as proxies for his will, often sending them to meet face-to-face with his most devoted followers; the dwarves.

Activity:

It is known that Volkanus created the dwarves shortly after he ascended, and they venerate him as their father.

Desired Behaviour in Followers:

Piety Benefits:

Earning and Losing Piety
 You increase your piety score to Volkanus when you expand the god’s influence in the world in a concrete way through acts such as these:
 - Fighting against those who would rule over others
 - Taking decisive action on impulse
 - Destroying something that has outlived its usefulness
 - Creating something wondrous in Volkanus’s name

Your piety score to Volkanus decreases if you diminish Volkanus’s influence in the world, work against freedom or self-expression, or allow tyranny to take hold through acts such as these:
 - Following an unjust law despite your misgivings
 - Creating something shoddy or flawed
 - Backing down from a fight or a contest

-Volkanus’s Devotee Piety 3+ Volkanus trait. As a devotee of Volkanus, you have drawn the notice of the forge god with your skill. You can cast shield of faith with this trait, requiring no material components, a number of times equal to your Intelligence modifier (minimum of once) and with spell slots. You regain all expended free uses when you finish a long rest.  In addition, you know the mending cantrip. Intelligence is your spellcasting ability for these spells.
 
 -Volkanus’s Votary Piety 10+ Volkanus trait. You can cast heat metal with this trait, requiring no material components. Once you cast the spell in this way, you can’t do so again until you finish a long rest, or if you use a spell slot. Intelligence is your spellcasting ability for this spell. In addition, you have advantage on saving throws against being knocked prone.
 
 -Volkanus’s Disciple Piety 25+ Volkanus trait. While you are standing and an effect would push you 1 foot or more, you can use your reaction to not be pushed, or to be pushed up to 50 feet, at your preference. Additionally, you can take 10 minutes to create any mundane item, providing you sacrifice an equivalent value of other items or coin.
 
-Champion of Mountains Piety 50+ Volkanus trait. You can increase your Strength or Intelligence score by 2 and also increase your maximum for that score by 2.
