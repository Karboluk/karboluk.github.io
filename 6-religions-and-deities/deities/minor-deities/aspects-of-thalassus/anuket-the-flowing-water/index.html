Anuket is a Minor God

Regional Names:

Achelus (Thracia), She Who Feeds the Sea

Domains:

Nature, Tempest

Regional Worship:

Not commonly worshiped, but with isolated shrines in river-bound fishing villages or small towns prone to flooding.

Nature:

Anuket, ironically given her role, is best described as "Still waters run deep". She will be calm and steady until the moment she flips; and either becomes very friendly or very hostile.

Her role is to bring people, material and ideas to her Master, Thalassus. This very functional role keeps her busy most of the time.

Associated Major Deity:

Thalassus of the Oceans

Relations to other Deities:

Origins:

When the Bassarian culture was at its height, and the King Apsu-Toli was most venerated by his people, some very strange things began to happen.

The first of these was the movement of the waters. Snow-capped mountains began to shed their white covering, and ice began to melt for the first time, and the water began to collect in lowlands. As this started to flood and overwhelm the residents of his country, King Apsu-Toli instructed his third minister, Karda Basmir, to fix the situation. He had no way of knowing this, but he had just decided on the ascent of the first mortal to become a Minor God.

After heading out, when Karda experienced the world's first river, she began to bend its course, trying to avoid the houses and farms that she could. This proved impossible at the time, and many drowned.

Activity:

Anuket brings everything she can to the realm of her master, which keeps her busy most of the time. To aid her, she invites natural spirits to occupy each river and stream, which has resulted in the Naiad population we see today.

Anuket herself works mostly as an administrator, echoing her former mortal life, hearing the petitions of her Naiads as mortals affect the rivers.

In recent times, Anuket suffered greatly in the Fall of Cimmeria, being grievously wounded by the impact- collateral damage rather than an intentional target. Thalassus looks to restore her, so she now recuperates in the bay that resulted by the fall, unknowingly entrapping Jaraxx, the Rot.

Her absence since then has resulted in more river-work industry among the mortals, and a decided increase in hostility in the Naiad.
