Aethenius is a Minor God

Regional Names:

Ptolemus (Thracia), He Who Brings the World to Him.

Domains:

Arcana, Knowledge, Order.

Regional Worship:

Statues of Aethenius as a wise owl, with or without horns, are often found at archives, libraries and other places of collected knowledge, but it has been so long since an active worship of him existed that it's likely that the architect of those places simply see them as appropriate dressing, ignorant of the religious meaning.

Nature:

Aethenius is very bright, very eager, and incredibly harsh with those who lie or hold back knowledge. He collects knowledge on every subject,

His normal form, an owl with stag horns, has not been seen in the world for some ages.

Associated Major Deity:

Thalassus of the Oceans

Origins:

When the Bassarian culture was at its height, and the King Apsu-Toli was most venerated by his people, some very strange things began to happen.

After Karda Basmir, who became Anuket, failed to stop the moving water and did not return, King Apsu-Toli set his highest sage to discover the reason for the change and develop a plan to end the issue. This sage, Apkallu who Reads, buried himself in the words of the most eminent philosophers, priests and magicians of the land, sending a thousand scribes out of the city of Bassaria to the distant corners of the kingdom.

The more he learned, and the more desperate he became to give King Apsu-Toli a solution, the more he isolated himself. His form changed as he had been chosen as the second mortal to ascend
to become a Minor God.

Activity:

Aethenius spends all of his time at his Library, The Aethenium, the location of which is a closely guarded secret. There he gathers, sort and hoards knowledge.
