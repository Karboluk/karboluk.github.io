###### Regional Worship: 
Among human cultures, worship of Volkanus is generally either as a Fire Cult ([[Thracia\|Thracia]]), individual eccentrics, or a proper human-lead church as in [[4. Geography and Politics/3. Among dwarves, he is venerated as their father; they work tirelessly to create makings worthy of him.

Gnomes aren't uncommon in either the human or dwarven worship scenarios, as their natural curiosity lends well to the practice. In both cases, though, they seem to succumb to the more manic expressions of interest and faith.

## Nature:

Volkanus is the god of the forge, the restless earth, and fire. He rules the raw creative force that infuses sapient minds. Volkanus is also the god of artisans, obsession, mania, and the cycle of creation and destruction.
 
As a forge radiates heat in the area around it, Volkanus’s influence provides inspiration to mortals. He makes exquisitely crafted objects almost constantly, sometimes absentmindedly working while he holds conversations with the other gods, only to destroy the finished product and begin again. Impulsive and mercurial, Volkanus is prone to bouts of either joyous productivity or frustrated anger. He often feels constrained by the limits of imagination, yearning to realize ideas that seem just out of reach.
 
Volkanus’s preferred form is that of a muscular man whose coal-hued skin is mostly covered in mutable organic bronze. He might also appear in the form of a fiery phoenix or a bull made of cooling lava, and for that reason, both of those creatures are associated with him. When angered, he might appear as an enormous mass of lava, a blazing fire, or a volcanic eruption. Mortals who see Volkanus in one of those forms seldom live to tell about it.

## Relations to other Deities:

Volkanus, nearly alone among the gods, is an advocate for [[6. Religions and Deities/Deities/Major Deities/Zatar the Ravaging Storm\|Zatar the Ravaging Storm]]. I assume it's because the destruction leaves so many more pieces for Volkanus to work with.

Volkanus and [[6. Religions and Deities/Deities/Major Deities/Thalassus of the Oceans\|Thalassus]] are in active opposition, and their followers will sometimes become violent.
## Origins:

Volkanus arose in Thracia during the height of [[6. Religions and Deities/Deities/Major Deities/Maahes of Shield and Blade\|Maahes]]' influence, as the land was being shattered and splintered by his presence. In a relatively peaceful [[6. Religions and Deities/Ascent to Godhood\|Ascent to Godhood]], the stories say that the large volcano, named for the God, of course, that grew between the warring cities of [[Agaria\|Agaria]] and [[Brekaris\|Brekaris]] was the beginning of the end for Maahes's time on this realm; when he saw how much damage he was causing to such an inferior people, his shame demanded his retreat. Volkanus rose up, and [[The Shield of Thracia\|The Shield of Thracia]], the mountain range now present, sealed almost a year of peace. 

Land was filled in and several nearby islands were rejoined to the mainland, which likely began the feud with [[6. Religions and Deities/Deities/Major Deities/Thalassus of the Oceans\|Thalassus]], but Volkanus did not remain on this realm in person. He uses his [[6. Religions and Deities/Deities/Minor Gods\|Minor Gods]] and Aspects as proxies for his will, often sending them to meet face-to-face with his most devoted followers; the dwarves.



## Activity:

It is known that Volkanus created the dwarves shortly after he ascended, and they venerate him as their father.

## Desired Behaviour in Followers:


### [[50. Glossary and Administrative/Player Character Options and Rules/Rules/Piety - Optional Rule\|Piety]] Benefits:

Earning and Losing Piety  
 You increase your piety score to Volkanus when you expand the god’s influence in the world in a concrete way through acts such as these:  
 - Fighting against those who would rule over others
 - Taking decisive action on impulse
 - Destroying something that has outlived its usefulness
 - Creating something wondrous in Volkanus’s name

 Your piety score to Volkanus decreases if you diminish Volkanus’s influence in the world, work against freedom or self-expression, or allow tyranny to take hold through acts such as these:  
 - Following an unjust law despite your misgivings
 - Creating something shoddy or flawed
 - Backing down from a fight or a contest


 -**Volkanus’s Devotee** *Piety 3+ Volkanus trait*. As a devotee of Volkanus, you have drawn the notice of the forge god with your skill. You can cast _shield of faith_ with this trait, requiring no material components, a number of times equal to your Intelligence modifier (minimum of once) and with spell slots. You regain all expended free uses when you finish a long rest.  In addition, you know the _mending_ cantrip. Intelligence is your spellcasting ability for these spells.  
 
 **-Volkanus’s Votary**  *Piety 10+ Volkanus trait*. You can cast _heat metal_ with this trait, requiring no material components. Once you cast the spell in this way, you can’t do so again until you finish a long rest, or if you use a spell slot. Intelligence is your spellcasting ability for this spell. In addition, you have advantage on saving throws against being knocked prone.  
 
 **-Volkanus’s Disciple** *Piety 25+ Volkanus trait*. While you are standing and an effect would push you 1 foot or more, you can use your reaction to not be pushed, or to be pushed up to 50 feet, at your preference. Additionally, you can take 10 minutes to create any mundane item, providing you sacrifice an equivalent value of other items or coin.
 
**-Champion of Mountains** *Piety 50+ Volkanus trait*. You can increase your Strength or Intelligence score by 2 and also increase your maximum for that score by 2.
