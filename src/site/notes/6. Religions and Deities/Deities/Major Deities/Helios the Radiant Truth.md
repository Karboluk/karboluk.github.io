Lawbringers ([[Thracia\|Thracia]]). Most current mortals worship his [[6. Religions and Deities/Deities/Minor Gods\|Aspects]] rather than Helios directly.



## Nature:

[[5. History and Myths/Luxan/The Luxan Empire\|The Luxan]]'s eponymous God of the Sun, Helios is the paragon of truth, justice, and beauty. He believes in progress, the improvement of mortal lives and the progression of their collective skills. Helios and his coterie, the Muses, are directly involved in the arts, medicine, and the ingenuity of invention.

Helios has a deep and unflagging love of mortals, to the point that I suspect he created them to begin with. For this reason, he is one of the few [[6. Religions and Deities/Deities/Major Gods\|Major Gods]] active in the mortal realm (through his muses and other agents, and his own distant presence, of course). Helios often interferes to change things as he thinks they should be changed; or enlists mortal heroes to do the same, whether they are willing or not.

## Origins:

The second oldest of the [[6. Religions and Deities/Deities/Major Gods\|Major Gods]], it's known from archaeological evidence that Helios was venerated by [[5. History and Myths/Luxan/The Luxan Empire\|The Luxan Empire]], meaning he predates the first major civilization. Given his remit, it is likely that he gifted civilization to mortals, and [[5. History and Myths/Luxan/The Luxan Empire\|The Luxan Empire]] was the result.  

Helios, unlike the [[6. Religions and Deities/Deities/Elder Gods/The Earth Mother Irithiel\|Earth Mother]] and the other [[6. Religions and Deities/Deities/Elder Gods\|Elder Gods]], is known to have Ascended, which is why he is classified as a [[6. Religions and Deities/Deities/Major Gods\|Major God]]; [[6. Religions and Deities/Deities/Minor Deities/Aspects of The Earth Mother/The Winds/The Winds\|the Winds]] have made this fact clear, but refuse to discuss it further. Due to the passage of time and the [[Sundering of the Lands\|Sundering of the Lands]], there is no record of the catastrophe caused by his [[6. Religions and Deities/Ascent to Godhood\|Ascent to Godhood]], but the lines of power that run below the ancient Luxan sites may be evidence of what happened given their veneration of the Sun God.

It is generally assumed that Helios is, in all real meanings of the word, [[the Sun\|the Sun]].

## Desired Behaviour in Followers:
Helios rewards ambition, the pursuit of knowledge, the pursuit of justice and the appreciation for the good things in life. 

### [[50. Glossary and Administrative/Player Character Options and Rules/Rules/Piety - Optional Rule\|Piety]] Benefits:
You increase your piety score to Helios when you expand the god’s influence in the world in a concrete way through acts such as these:

- Carrying out punishment on a fugitive from justice
- Exacting vengeance for a significant wrong done to you
- Defending a settlement from attacking monsters
- Commissioning great works of Art
- Taking a great personal risk to discover something

 Your piety score to Helios decreases if you diminish Helios’s influence in the world, contradict his ideals, or make him look weak and cowardly through acts such as these:

- Breaking an explicit promise or oath
- Violating any just law
- Putting others at risk through your own cowardice
- Rejection of a path forward because the potential for harm

-**Helios’s Devotee** _Piety 3+ Helios trait_. As a devotee of Helios, you have proven yourself a worthy champion of the sun god. You can call on Helios’s favor and cast _bless_ with this trait, requiring no material components. Helios’s blessing manifests as a nimbus around the affected creatures, causing them to shed dim light in a 5-foot radius until the spell ends. You can cast the spell in this way a number of times equal to your Wisdom modifier (minimum of once) or with spell slots. You regain all expended free uses when you finish a long rest. Wisdom is your spellcasting ability for this spell. 
-**Helios’s Votary** _Piety 10+ Helios trait_ You can cast _daylight_ with this trait once, or with spell slots. You regain your free use when you finish a long rest. Wisdom is your spellcasting ability for this spell. In addition, you have advantage on saving throws against being frightened. 
-**Helios’s Disciple** _Piety 25+ Helios trait_ You grow accustomed to the blazing radiance of the sun; you have advantage on saving throws against being blinded, and you have resistance to fire damage. 
-**Champion of the Sun** _Piety 50+ Helios trait_ You can increase your Strength or Wisdom score by 2 and also increase your maximum for that score by 2.
