## Nature:

Rrakash, the God of Pain, appears as a creature of yellow energy, whose form contains and consumes a caged humanoid form semi-visible within.

Rrakash is a new god, and has taken to appearing in a seemingly random fashion. His victims are not always killed, but those that survive generally wish they hadn't. Divinations describe the attacks as "feedings".

## Origins:

Born in anticipation and parellel to the [[6. Religions and Deities/Ascent to Godhood\|ascent]] of [[6. Religions and Deities/Deities/Major Deities/Chorai the Stillborn Dead\|Chorai]], Rrakash, then [[Esiphin Steelbender\|Esiphin Steelbender]], was the experimental partner the necromancer [[Soza Hakade\|Soza Hakade]], who later would become Chorai. Their experiments resulted in marked improvements in the field of arcane cloning.  Meeting academic rejection instead of the praise she'd anticipated, Soza turned her studies further to the predictable (and honestly quite trite) goal of achieving lichdom. Esiphin balked at this and left the study of magic entirely.

But each had created hundreds of clones. 

Esiphin, neglecting this, perhaps pretending it didn't happen- who can say- eventually dies of natural causes. And then, for centuries, was reborn again and again every six months. 

Sometimes he drowned in his cloning pod. Sometimes he was killed by their laboratory's defenses. Sometimes by the throng of undead (former clones) who had naturally remained nearby in their hidden facility. 

But he remembered it all, and went quite mad. The necromancy twisted over the years, he is empowered by this pain and seeks to experience as much as possible; himself or vicariously through others. 

Known only through divination and by a few who have been unfortunate enough to meet him, Rrakash's [[6. Religions and Deities/Ascent to Godhood\|Ascent to Godhood]] seems to have occurred in Limbo, on the border to the Astral Plane. Given the timeless nature of those places, the consequences are not yet known; but with Limbo, anything is possible.

No [[6. Religions and Deities/Deities/Minor Gods\|Minor Gods]] or Aspects have emerged relating to Rrakash.
