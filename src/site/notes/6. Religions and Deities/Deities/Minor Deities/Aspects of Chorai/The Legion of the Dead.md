The Legion of the Dead is a [[6. Religions and Deities/Deities/Minor Gods\|Minor God]]

###### Regional Names: 
The Army Myriad, [[Soza Hakade\|Soza Hakade]] (mistaken), She Who is Unending
###### Domains: 
Arcana, Death

###### Regional Worship: 
None



## Nature:

The Legion walks the world alone; though the name makes it sound like there are many, there is only one at a time (barring exceptional circumstances). 


## Associated Major Deity:
[[6. Religions and Deities/Deities/Major Deities/Chorai the Stillborn Dead\|Chorai the Stillborn Dead]]

## Relations to other Deities:

Acquainted with [[6. Religions and Deities/Deities/Minor Deities/Aspects of the Fates/Fantis, the Weaver of Beads\|Fantis, the Weaver of Beads]] and [[6. Religions and Deities/Deities/Minor Deities/Aspects of Astere/Paratiritis, the Seeing\|Paratiritis, the Seeing]], and on reasonable terms with both.


## Origins:

Born in anticipation of the [[6. Religions and Deities/Ascent to Godhood\|ascent]] of [[6. Religions and Deities/Deities/Major Deities/Chorai the Stillborn Dead\|Chorai]], the legion is the result of the mortal efforts of the necromancer [[Soza Hakade\|Soza Hakade]], who later would become Chorai. Her experiments with her partner [[Esiphin Steelbender\|Esiphin Steelbender]] (who later became [[6. Religions and Deities/Deities/Major Deities/Rrakash of Tears and Torment\|Rrakash]]) resulted in marked improvements in the field of arcane cloning.  Meeting academic rejection instead of the praise she'd anticipated, Soza turned her studies further to the predictable (and honestly quite trite) goal of achieving lichdom. Esiphin balked at this and left the study of magic entirely.

Soza, after finding the demilich remains of [[5. History and Myths/Cimmeria/Quidun the Wise and Benificent\|Quidun the Wise and Benificent]], gained the secrets of permanent unlife and achieved lichdom- see the entry on [[6. Religions and Deities/Deities/Major Deities/Chorai the Stillborn Dead\|Chorai]] for the rest of that story. 

But a sharp mind may ask- what happened to her clones once she abandoned mortality? 

It seems that her clones, after some time, gained sentience of their own. The cloning pods moved to follow the undead necromancer, though she could not see or feel them. 

The first few clones lived out lives in [[Sendria\|Sendria]], with three out of four being persecuted for witchcraft in that backward place. Eventually the fifth clone emerged (naming itself Evette Blackthorn), and being in a strange situation with no memory, took it upon herself to escape. Fleeing to [[Port Rain\|Port Rain]], she eventually discovered her past, made allies (see [[The Seekers\|The Seekers]]) and was part of the events that hampered the [[6. Religions and Deities/Ascent to Godhood\|ascent]] of Soza, resulting in her becoming [[6. Religions and Deities/Deities/Major Deities/Chorai the Stillborn Dead\|Chorai the Stillborn Dead]] rather than another, more active form of godhood.

But the energies already present in the clones, as well as what the clone Evette drew from the [[5. History and Myths/Other Places/Obelisks of Power\|Obelisks of Power]] that the Seekers encountered, finalised her position as a [[6. Religions and Deities/Deities/Minor Gods\|Minor God]]. 



## Activity:

The legion, still Evette Blackthorn at time of writing, wanders the land, studying and learning. She has found more of her future self in a few places, and has come to terms with the fact that there will never be an end to this. She has, at the least, solved the memory loss on transferal and had previously made contact with her past selves; but one wonders how much of her mortal soul will remain after a few centuries, a few millennia, a few ages.
