You can craft items of any type, and can enchant items quickly and to a high standard. All you need are the raw materials, a suitable workspace, and time. You gain proficiency in Arcana.

### Crafting Time
#### Adventuring
Assuming a normal adventuring day of 8 hours, such as when travelling on the road, you may craft for up to 4 hours and still achieve an 8 hour long rest, or longer if you require less rest or no rest. If you do not sleep, you may craft for the entire rest period should you wish.

Crafting while adventuring is situationally dependant; you may have time before going to sleep, but crafting is not usually a quiet affair. If you are making anything with stone, gem, or metal, you automatically fail any Dexterity (Stealth) checks you attempt. Regardless of what you are making, you are also very focused; you are surprised and armed only with the crafting tools you are using if initiative is called.

If you choose to craft for longer than described above, you must succeed on a DC 15 Constitution saving throw or start the following day with one level of exhaustion.

#### Downtime
Crafting during downtime can occupy up to 16 hours of your day, if you undertake no other activities; including eating. For each other activity you undertake, one hour is deducted from the maximum as you need to find your spot again. 
	If you spend more than 14 hours crafting in a downtime day, you must succeed on a DC 15 Constitution saving throw or start the following day with one level of exhaustion.


### Non-Magical Equipment:

#### Adventuring Gear, Weapons, Armor of Mundane Quality.

##### Cost
Appropriate materials equal to one quarter of the item cost must be provided- you can improvise the rest.
##### Time
Roll Intelligence (Arcana), with advantage if the appropriate tools and materials are present. Divide the item cost in gp by the result of this roll: this is how many minutes it takes you to craft the item (minimum 1 minute). 
	In the case of items with costs below 1 gp, such as candles, you can craft them in batches using a contraption you improvise, creating up to 20 per minute. No roll is required.

#### Adventuring Gear, Weapons, Armor of Superior Quality.

When crafting equipment made from exotic materials, such as Mithril, Adamantine, [[ZZZ. Private DM Info/49. Secrets/Materials/Ever-Ore\|Ever-Ore]], [[ZZZ. Private DM Info/49. Secrets/Materials/Infernal Iron\|Blacksteel]], [[ZZZ. Private DM Info/49. Secrets/Materials/Demonic Alloy\|Flux]], Dragon Hide, etc, you must take more time to ensure the base material is not damaged. 
##### Cost
For items with normal gp costs below 300 gp:
	No additional costs are required, unless the amount of exotic material is miniscule.
For items with normal gp costs 300 and above:
	If enough of the exotic material is available to physically craft the desired item, there is no additional cost. 
	If there isn't, materials equal to half of the normal item cost must be provided as additional precautions must be taken.
		If you're unsure if there are adequate exotic materials, roll Intelligence(Arcana), with advantage if the appropriate tools are present, against a DC of the base item cost divided by 30. On a success, no additional materials are required.
##### Time
Roll Intelligence (Arcana), with advantage if the appropriate tools and mundane materials are present. Divide the mundane item cost in gp by twice the result of this roll (round up): this is how many hours it takes you to craft the item. Another creature who is proficient in the relevant tool can roll an Arcana check (DC 15), or Sleight of Hand (DC 15), as appropriate based on the task, to aid and speed things up. A roll of less than 5 adds another required hour instead. Another creature with the Crafty feat does not need to make this roll. 
	The other character needs to succeed on this check as you will be working on 10 parts of the project at once and keeping up with you is confusing and taxing for most people.
	If your original roll produces a result less than 1, then a fraction of an hour is taken, with a minimum of 1 minute (0.167 of an hour). 
		For items with gp costs below 1, you can spend 1 minute creating a batch of 5 items with no roll needed.

#### Basic Potions and Poisons
Assuming the relevant tools and materials are available, the creation of Potions of Healing, Alchemist's Fire, Acid Flasks, Poison, and Antitoxin take 1 hour and cost 1/8th the normal cost. No roll is required.

Unless you are a Cleric or a Paladin, you are unable to make Holy Water.

#### Spell Scrolls
Unless you are a wizard or have the scribe background, you use the normal rules for scribing spell scrolls, as you are more aligned with invention than wizardry. For convenience, they are summarized here:

1. The relevant spell must be prepared each day the work is being done
2. Material Components must be present, and are consumed when the scroll is finished if applicable. 
3. Cantrips are cast as if the user is the same level you were when you created the scroll.
4. Spells use your Spell Attack modifier and Spell Save DC from when you completed the scroll.

#### Magical Items

Providing the appropriate base materials are available, you can craft magical items according to the following table. If the item being crafted has a normal purchase cost (weapon, armor, tool or adventuring gear), the item must already be available or crafted first, using the rules detailed above. 

If the item being created allows the user to cast a spell, You must know the spell and have it prepared during times you are crafting. Unless you are a cleric or paladin, you are unable to craft items that are explicitly divine, such as a *Holy Avenger*, but you may be able to replicate many of the same effects in your own way; such as using extraplanar materials to ward off specific creatures. Discuss this with your DM.

##### Cost
The costs listed in the table below represent esoteric goods, magical materials or other hard-to-find substances. There is a 75% chance that they are available in a city, or a 25% chance that they are available in  a large town. They will be unavailable in a smaller settlement. 

##### Secret Ingredients
Some items also require specific ingredients; for example, a Potion of Giant Strength requires the toenail of the appropriate type of giant. Other special ingredients include specific substances, such as Mithril, Adamantine, [[ZZZ. Private DM Info/49. Secrets/Materials/Ever-Ore\|Ever-Ore]], [[ZZZ. Private DM Info/49. Secrets/Materials/Infernal Iron\|Blacksteel]], Darkmetal or Dragon Scales; or specific ingredients such as Kraken Ink or Blood, Unicorn Horn, the Blood of a God, pure elemental fire, [[ZZZ. Private DM Info/49. Secrets/Materials/Demonic Alloy\|Flux]], etc. These special ingredients must be supplied on top of the listed cost and must be available throughout crafting. If they are lost, they must be replaced before crafting can resume.


##### Specific Circumstances
Some items must also be crafted in specific circumstances or in specific locations. This will be known before crafting begins and does not add to the time or cost, but may limit the amount of time you can spend in a given day crafting. One example is that a Moon Sickle or other Moon-Touched weapon must be created under the light of a full moon, so only 12 hours of crafting are possible in a given month, assuming the skies are clear.

##### Time
Work is performed in hour long segments; you must have a workspace and the materials at hand. If you are in danger, such as trying to craft during active combat or in a hazardous environment, you must make a Constitution or Intelligence saving throw (your choice) at the end of each hour or that hour does not count towards completion. 

When crafting magical items, you may be assisted by someone with the appropriate tool proficiency providing they succeed on an Intelligence (Arcana) check DC 20, reducing the remaining time by half of the time they spend. If their total is 9 or lower, they add one hour instead. Another creature with the Crafty feat may assist with no roll, as long as they have the requisite tool proficiency.

##### Consumables
For consumable items, such as potions, one-use tokens, or wands that do not recharge; the time and cost are halved.
