Chiriki is a [[VR372/5. Religions and Deities/Deities/Minor Gods-VR372\|Minor God]].

###### Regional Names: 
Atalanta ([[Thracia-VR372\|Thracia]]), Siki of Spear and Bow (Goliath of [[VR372/4. Geography, Lands and Countries/1. The Continent of the Shining Vale/2. Others/2. Subdurni/The Wastes of Subdurni-VR372\|The Wastes of Subdurni]]), She Who Stalks and Pounces
###### Domains: 
Nature, Trickery, War

###### Regional Worship: 

Worship of Chiriki is often cult-like and can sometimes be confused with worship of Baphomet by those too lazy to pay attention to detail. 

In [[Thracia-VR372\|Thracia]], Chiriki (as Atalanta) has temples where mainly women, but still some men, serve in a state of celibacy. These Vestal Priestesses and Priests do not participate in hunts outside of ritual, but serve as advisors and strategists in war. 


## Nature:

Chiriki is a quiet goddess, but aggressively quiet rather than bashfully. She watches and waits for her moment to act; which means that, while other gods may dabble and interfere more, Chiriki's plans almost always work- and swiftly and efficiently.

Chiriki's [[VR372/5. Religions and Deities/Ascent to Godhood-VR372\|ascent]] is known to have happened (from [[VR372/5. Religions and Deities/Deities/Minor Deities/Aspects of The Earth Mother/The Winds/The Winds-VR372\|The Winds]]). It occurred after the world existed and the [[VR372/5. ## Activity:

Chiriki stays out of mortal affairs for the most part, but will interfere in the natural world to correct an imbalance, remove an unnatural threat, or sometimes to create a new hunting ground by altering the terrain.

When she does choose to interact with mortals, it is often to punish rather than to aid. She seems to have no qualms about poor hunters starving, or wild beasts destroying farms and villages. There are a few records, however, of times when mortals living in harmony with the land began to gain magical powers which let them thrive, and also to fend off more powerful and civilized peoples. 

An exception to this behaviour is, of course, in [[Thracia-VR372\|Thracia]], where worship of Chiriki (as Atalanta) is a very civilized and urban affair. For those of us outside that archipelago, it seems that she perhaps tolerates this as a way to learn how to hunt humans- given the tactical and military purposes that the Vestals are put to. The [[Thracia-VR372\|Thracians]] simply have a different view of her, it seems, noting her keen eye and quick mind, and associating her to the owl, hawk or cat, but without the common hatred of civilization.

Aside from any actions she may choose to take, The Huntress is always stalking something, somewhere in the world. I think the only beast she hasn't yet challenged is [[The Torrasque-VR372\|The Torrasque]], and it's doubtful that this exception is from lack of courage. 

*I can not state this with certainty, but a rumour has surfaced that Chiriki's unwillingness to deal with mortals is due to the fact that she was an early patron of the humans who would go on to become the [[Yuan-Ti-VR372\|Yuan-Ti]]. Perhaps she feels her attempt to guide them failed, and has simply moved to easier targets since then.
-Reyvan*


## Desired Behaviour in Followers:


### [[VR372/50. Appendix 1,  Rules/Piety - Optional Rule-VR372\|Piety]] Benefits:

You increase your piety score to Chiriki when you reinforce the goddess’s influence in the world in a concrete way through acts such as these:

- Hunting a worthy quarry to completion, for food and glory
- Punishing those who hunt for profit, or who hunt excessively
- Proving your worth in a contest of hunting skills, such as archery
- Slaying an aberration, a fiend, or an undead in the name of Chiriki

Your piety score to Chiriki decreases if you diminish Chiriki’s influence in the world by encouraging civilization, failing to punish those who exploit nature, or offend her sensibilities through acts such as these:

- Killing an animal and not making use of it.
- Dedicating a building or making an animal sacrifice to any god, including Chiriki, except as Atalanta.
- Protecting a city or farm from natural dangers

-**Chiriki’s Devotee** _Piety 3+ Chiriki trait_. As a devotee of Chiriki, you have proven yourself a predator of the wilds. You can cast _hunter’s mark_ with this trait a number of times equal to your Wisdom modifier (minimum of once) or with spell slots. You regain all expended uses when you finish a long rest. Wisdom is your spellcasting ability for this spell. 

-**Chiriki’s Votary** _Piety 10+ Chiriki trait_. You can cast _speak with animals_ with this trait a number of times equal to your Wisdom modifier (minimum of once), or with spell slots. If you can already speak with animals, you instead gain *beast bond.* You regain all expended uses when you finish a long rest. Wisdom is your spellcasting ability for this spell. In addition, you have advantage on saving throws against being poisoned and resistance to poison damage.

-**Chiriki’s Disciple** _Piety 25+ Chiriki trait_. You can now hide when only partially obscured, or , if you already had this ability, you can hide when unobscured as long as you are in a natural environment. You lose the benefit of hiding when you make an attack, dash, or cast a spell requiring verbal components other than *hunter's mark, speak with animals* or *beast bond*. 

-**Champion of the Hunt** _Piety 50+ Chiriki trait_. You can increase your Dexterity or Wisdom score by 2 and also increase your maximum for that score by 2.
