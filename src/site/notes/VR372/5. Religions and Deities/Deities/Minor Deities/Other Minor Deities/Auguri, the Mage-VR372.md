## Nature:

Auguri is fascinated by the progression of mortals, particularly humans, when it comes to their overreach with magic. His past history has informed his opinion that wizards should co-operate rather than compete.

Auguri appears usually as a human man in his early 20s with wavy blond hair, wearing fine but not ostentatious clothes.

## Relations to other Deities:

## Origins:

Auguri was born Caleb Druth, a minor noble in the [[Cimmerian Empire-VR372\|Cimmerian Empire]]. Learning magic from a young age, he became a prodigious inventor of new spells- and indeed a new explorative field of magic, [[Chronomancy-VR372\|Chronomancy]].  When [[VR372/4. Geography, Lands and Countries/1. The Continent of the Shining Vale/2. Others/4. Cimmeria/Quidun the Wise and Beneficent-VR372\|Quidun]] fell and [[VR372/4. Geography, Lands and Countries/1. The Continent of the Shining Vale/2. Others/4. Cimmeria/Isthariel the Channeler-VR372\|Isthariel]] ascended to power, Caleb began intense study on how best to protect himself from political movements like this- his conclusion being, in the end, that if they could be foreseen and/or made as if they never happened, the outcome would be better than if they were punished or blocked. His work towards this goal proceeded until, after the period of Isthariel's exile, she returned as [[VR372/5. Religions and Deities/Deities/Major Deities/Zatar the Ravaging Storm-VR372\|Zatar]]. 

Caleb's preparations interfaced strangely with the divine magic surrounding the newly born Goddess of Carnage, and instead of reversing the causal events it pulled Caleb into them. Drawing a portion of the power of Zatar's [[VR372/5. Religions and Deities/Ascent to Godhood-VR372\|ascent]], Caleb became Auguri, the Mage. He attempted to use his new powers to stop the [[Fall of Cimmeria-VR372\|Fall of Cimmeria]], but was simply overpowered; once again his preparations saved him, preventing his destruction or punishment at the hands of Zatar.

## Activity:

Auguri has set up towers in many places across the world's surface and underworld. These towers exist in fixed locations, but span vast lengths of time- themselves existing at all moments across that timespan simultaneously. They are not difficult to enter, but generally require some simple item or token to prevent someone stumbling in, and individuals are only allowed to enter once in their life, to avoid the paradox of entering the same extra-temporal space, in the same location, simultaneously.

He invites aspiring wizards in, and trains them for years or decades. His magic is such that they can be returned to the entry point at any point in the timeline they choose; but being clever enough to not anger [[VR372/5. Religions and Deities/Deities/Major Deities/The Fates/The Fates-VR372\|The Fates]], Auguri abides by the rules of causality with strict attention and teaches his students to do the same. In the rare event he finds a guest that he trusts with this responsibility, he may make an exception and allow them to return before they left; instructing them to make no changes to events that they know to have happened. 

In his towers, and for the same reason, guests are kept segregated so none may inform another of what they know of history- for one's history could easily be another's future, and then they are bound to that causal chain.

Auguri's interventions, under many aliases, are known to have resulted in the creation of the following arcane organizations:

- [[The Magesterium Panopticon-VR372\|The Magesterium Panopticon]]
- [[The Order of Yellow-VR372\|The Order of Yellow]]
- [[The Thracian Anti-Witchcraft Alliance-VR372\|The Thracian Anti-Witchcraft Alliance]]
- [[C.A.L.I.S.T.O-VR372\|C.A.L.I.S.T.O]]

*This list, which I can not ever enumerate as it may grow or shrink over time depending on what will have been done in the future-present, is presumed to be complete. I gave this a great deal of thought, and I have accepted that I have limitations in this regard.
-Reyvan*

These organizations each, in their own way, do serve to prevent the internecine magical warfare that Auguri was afraid of; but once inevitably taken over by mortals, some have twisted their original purpose and become magocratic political powers, sometimes competing with each other in the very way he was trying to avoid. Being in hiding as he is, he has not had the opportunity to make any further corrections.



## Desired Behaviour in Followers:


### [[VR372/50. Appendix 1,  Rules/Piety - Optional Rule-VR372\|Piety]] Benefits:

Clerics of Auguri may use Intelligence as their Cleric spellcasting ability, and/or Wisdom for Wizard spellcasting if they take both classes. Auguri can teach many spells and powers not normally available to players.
