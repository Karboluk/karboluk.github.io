Parvati is a [[VR372/5. Religions and Deities/Deities/Minor Gods-VR372\|Minor God]].

###### Domains: 
Life, Light, Peace

###### Regional Worship: 
Worship of Parvati is generally in the building of community; therefore she is often worshiped alongside [[VR372/5. Religions and Deities/Deities/Minor Deities/Aspects of Volkanus/Lady Racca, the Planner-VR372\|Lady Racca]].



## Nature:

Parvati, she of open arms, is the Muse of Love. This encompasses romantic love, but also familial and community love, with a particular emphasis on tolerance, acceptance and forgiveness. 

Parvati wants the best for all mortals, and believes that if everyone accepted each other as they are, forgives others and themselves, and simply loves, that all problem and strife would soon end. 

*Because drought, flood and the actions of other gods, of course can be solved by wishful thinking. 
-Reyvan*

## Origins:

When the initial issues in [[Bassaria-VR372\|Bassaria]], following the [[Sundering of the Lands-VR372\|Sundering of the Lands]], had been addressed by [[VR372/5. Religions and Deities/Deities/Minor Deities/Aspects of Helios/Themis, the Righteous-VR372\|Themis]] and [[VR372/5. Religions and Deities/Deities/Minor Deities/Aspects of Helios/Aesthenes, the Glorious-VR372\|Aesthenes]], [[VR372/5. Religions and Deities/Deities/Major Deities/Helios the Radiant Truth-VR372\|Helios]] saw the people harbouring lingering resentments, jealousy over the newfound beautiful things they had made, and beginning to fracture into tribes. Wanting to revive the glory of Bassaria, Helios wished for the people to accept and forgive the past, so as to be able to make a common future: thus was born Parvati, the very idea of Love.


## Activity:

Parvati visits the various people of the world in many guises, de-escalating conflict and building community wherever she goes. Along with [[VR372/5. Religions and Deities/Deities/Minor Deities/Aspects of Volkanus/Lady Racca, the Planner-VR372\|Lady Racca]], she will help communities build common ground, literally and metaphorically.
